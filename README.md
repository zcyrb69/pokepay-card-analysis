## Why "No Annual Fee" Actually Matters for Crypto Cards

A traditional premium credit card can justify a $95–$550 annual fee with airport lounges, travel credits, and concierge services. Crypto cards are a different animal. Most of them are prepaid or debit instruments—you load your own crypto, you spend your own crypto, and the issuer makes money on conversion spreads and top-up fees rather than revolving interest. Paying an annual fee on top of that is essentially paying twice.

That is why the cards that have gained real traction in 2026 are the ones that dropped the annual fee to zero:

- **Coinbase Card** — no annual fee, no spending fee, US-only for rewards.
- **Bybit Card** — no annual fee, no hidden fees, 0.5% FX on top of Mastercard's rate.
- **Gemini Card** — no annual fee, category-based crypto rewards.
- **MetaMask Card (Virtual)** — no annual or maintenance fees; the Metal tier does charge $199/year but bundles perks.
- **PokePay** — no annual fee, no monthly fee, no micro-transaction fee, no inactivity fee.

The pattern is clear: annual fees are becoming the exception, not the rule. The real cost of a crypto card now hides in three less obvious places—**top-up fees, spending fees, and FX fees**. A card that advertises "zero annual fee" can still cost you 2–3% per transaction if you do not read the fine print. Keep that lens in mind for the rest of this guide.

## The Real Cost of Using PokePay—Beyond the "Zero Annual Fee" Headline

Here is where most reviews get vague and most users get surprised. PokePay's fee structure is transparent, but the components stack:

- **1% top-up fee** every time you load USDT, USDC, BTC, or ETH onto the card.
- **1% spending fee** on every purchase.
- **1% FX fee** when the transaction currency is not HKD (the base currency of the HK-issued card).

So if you top up $500 in USDT and then spend it in USD on a foreign-merchant site, you pay roughly **$5 on the top-up, $5 on the spend, and $5 on the FX conversion—about $15, or 3%, on a single $500 transaction**. For occasional spend—paying for ChatGPT Plus once a month, renewing a domain, buying a Steam alternative game—that is acceptable. For daily or high-volume spend, it compounds quickly and you will feel it by month three.

This is the single most important thing to internalize: **"no annual fee" does not mean "no fee."** It means the fee has been moved from a fixed yearly charge to a per-transaction charge. Whether that is a good trade depends entirely on how often you swipe.
